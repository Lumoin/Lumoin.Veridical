Determinism: the harness injects a CounterRandomEngine whose k-th byte ever produced is (k & 0xFF).
The reference draws randomness in order: blinding rows (ILDT block, IDOT dblock, IQUAD dblock),
witness rows (r subfield padding per row, subfield_boundary = nw), quadratic triples (3*r per
triple), then the Merkle nonces (one 32-byte nonce per leaf). The same stream is replayed in C#.

"c16_*" / "c32_*" lines are a deterministic commit over W[i] = of_scalar(i+1), one quadratic
constraint W[2] = W[0]*W[1], nw=8 nq=1 rateinv=4 nreq=2. The *_root is the Merkle root; *_leafN are
SHA256(nonce_N || to_bytes_field of column (dblock+N) over all nrow rows); the 16-byte little-endian
to_bytes_field framing is the wire format. *_prover_root_matches=1 confirms the self-contained
harness replica reproduces the real LigeroProver::commit() root.
